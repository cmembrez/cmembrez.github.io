Journey

Early on, I have been attracted by the creativity and logic offered by new technology. During my childhood, I enjoyed video games and computer. I created animations in Flash, developed maps for Half-Life with Hammer, learned some C++. To help my brother to give tutoring lessons, I created a website in PHP wtih a MySQL database to store the students logins and course informations. I played around with Photoshop to name just a few.

For a career start, I decided to take the road of economics and finance, as I appreciated the exchange and flow of funds in our current capitalist world. I was passionated by the use of logic, problem solving and modeling aspect with the use of mathematics applied to finance. In my first job in financial investment, I used the tools acquired to analyze and build new tools for my colleagues. I wrote a few scripts in VBA for Excel and in R. This last bit sparked the potential of new solutions using computer science. With always more noise around machine learning and artificial intelligence, I aimed for a break to study computer science in Montreal, Canada. But covid decided otherwise, and I stayed in Fribourg, Switzerland. I took most of my courses around computer vision and deep learning. But one project in video game development using a virtual reality headset changed everything.

This created a renewed awe to the use of new hardware and new technology to improve our way of doing things and discovering new ways. The new modalities from virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR) expends our possibilities to interact with new virtual worlds and our real world. I started by developing a VR adventure video game, followed by an AR serious game for people with visual impairments. In my first job as a software engineer, I explore the possibilities within a mix of industrial and healthcare contexts. In my current spare time, I explore new VR experiences and use-cases within other industries. -- computer vision: add somewhere the use of a pre trained model deployed on the edge, for a buttler robot.

The passion for computer science and coding and technology as always been around and always will be.
